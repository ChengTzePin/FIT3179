The Global HIV Statistics

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) is a type of virus that can caused Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). HIV is a virus that attacks cells that help the body fight infection with no cure but can be suppressed through an effective treatment called antiretroviral therapy (ART). AIDS is a type of disease that spread by contact with certain bodily fluids of a person with HIV, mostly through unprotected sex or sharing injection drug equipment, some also through mother-to-child when a pregnant woman is HIV positive.

How many new cases?

Over the time, the trend shows that, for almost every continent, the new cases per 1000 population increased. However, surprisingly, for the most affected continent, which is Africa, the trend shows that the new cases per 1000 population dropped.

How many are there in total?

The graph shows that the curve of the prevalence of HIV becomes smoother in year 2000 after a steep increase since 1990. However, the curve become steeper again after year 2008. Besides that, Africa Region has the highest prevalence of HIV at all time.

How many deaths?

The graph shows that the trend of number of death cases of HIV having a similar trend with the prevalence of HIV in the 1990s. However, the death cases reach its peak at around 2004 and 2005, then it started to decrease after that.

Which age group affected the most?

The graph once again shows that Africa region is once again the most affected region by HIV. It has not only the highest absolute death cases but also the highest death rates per 1000 population . The graph also shows that 15-49 years and 50-69 years are the 2 most affected age groups by HIV. Over time, the death rates of under 5 is getting lower while 50-69 years is getting higher.

Did ART saved more lives than HIV killed?

In year 2016, in Eastern Europe and Central Asia, Antiretroviral therapy has less effect where the deaths are still more than deaths averted. However, surprisingly, for East and Southern Africa, most of the countries are having deaths numbers lower than deaths averted. Overall, from year 2010 to 2016, the trend shows that ART is gradually improving on saving lives from HIV.
